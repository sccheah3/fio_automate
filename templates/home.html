Disk Benchmark Homepage

Any issues, contact stanleycheah@supermicro.com.

Drives: Displays all drives ever tested and uploaded. You can search and filter fields here to select different drives to compare their performances. Also, you can click on each drive and view all benchmarks done on it.

Convert Logs: This is where you can upload your raw fio result file and you will automatically download an excel sheet of your results charted and graphed. This will not save your results to the database, as raw fio results do not contain any useful information about the disk.

Automate FIO: Just enter in your BMC ip, BMC user/pass, LAN mac, cburn img (ex: cburn-r77) you want to use, and what burnin directory you want to save it to, just like autopxe. This will reboot your system and set the boot device to pxe. An autopxe command will be set once you submit the information.

Upload Logs: If you happen to do a manual run of fio and want to upload the logs to the database, you need two things: your fio result logs and a file that specifies your drive device slot, drive part number, and your drive firmware version. Keep in mind that it is case-sensitive.
Example: /dev/nvme0n1,Micron_9300_MTFDHAL7T6TDP,11300DN0
Each drive will be on a newline. The data for nvmes are gathered via nvme command. For sata/sas, it is through the smartctl command.
